                              dovyski Prasenjit Mondal • 2 years ago

                              Thanks!

                              The third vector I mentioned is the character's position (remember that the position is also a vector). All you have to do is update lineIntersectsCircle() to check if ahead, ahead2 and *position* are within the obstacle.

                              About the ahead vector coinciding with the obstacle center, the avoidance force will be zero indeed. That, however, only happens if the character remains in that position and you are not scaling the ahead vectors according to the character's current velocity (see "Improving Collision Detection").

                              The best solution for that is to replace my "ahead and ahead2" approach with a real line/circle intersection math code. It will solve all those problems and will give more accurate information about the collision.

                                                                                                dovyski Anon • 2 years ago

                                                                                                You are welcome! About your question, that's a tricky one :)

                                                                                                There is no easy way to make a character aware of the movement of its neighbors without adding a lot of code. If that was easy, you could "query" a nearby character to check his path, then you would follow a different one.

                                                                                                Without much code, the way I would fix that is by using some sort of variation for MAX_SEE_AHEAD for each character. That way when two characters are facing the same problem, they will act differently (depending on how much variation was added).

                                                                                                E.g. a character with MAX_SEE_AHEAD=80 will avoid obstacle much sooner than a character with MAX_SEE_AHEAD=40. It can make them follow different paths.

                                                                                                          Benoît Dumas dovyski • 2 years ago

                                                                                                          For me, the shaking is the result of the avoidance.
                                                                                                          - At update N, agent detects an obstacle and steer to avoid it.
                                                                                                          - At Update N+1, agent doesn't detect obstacle anymore and follow its other goals normally.
                                                                                                          - At update N+2, the obstacle is detected again and agent steer to avoid it.

                                                                                                          So shaking should be less important with a lesser avoidance force, but still exists if you steer out of the obstacle radius (which you normally want to do)

                                                                                                          The shaking should disapear if you compute the avoidance force after all other forces as been applied on its velocity this update.
