              dovyski Richard • 3 months ago

              Hello! You are welcome! I'm glad you like the tutorial :)

              In all the tutorials about steering behavior that I wrote, each entity has a single force (the velocity vector) to guide its movements. What you are describing here is the use of more of those vectors, something like a velocity vector for each thruster in the spaceship. The steering behavior code will remain the same, the only change you will have to do is to calculate N different steering forces instead of just one (pretty much looping the thrusters calculating the steering force). Each of those steering forces will be calculated based on a thruster.

              As a result, you will end up with several "see aheads", for instance, one for each of your velocity vectors (thrusters). In theory, since all steering behavior calculations are performed every frame, your spaceship will automatically adjust the velocity force of each thruster in order to pursue/avoid something (pretty much the way you described: a stronger thruster will have a bigger MAX_VELOCITY for its velocity vector, allowing that thruster to be more powerful). If you add all the resulting velocity vectors, you should have a single vector that will move the ship towards the goal (like following the instructions of all thrusters at the same time).

              Maybe you will have to invert all velocity vectors (multiply them by -1) after you calculate the steering forces, because that's how thruster propulsion work (they push the spaceship away from something in order to get to another place). In this tutorial, the steering force (and the velocity vector) point towards the target; if they were thrusters, they would move the ship away from its target. I don't know if I could make myself understood.

              If you have any question, just fire it up!
